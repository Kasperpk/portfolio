To write a book about data is not something new. There are numerous pieces of work out there and you might ask why this is different. I can't assure you that it is, or that it will give you new insights. One of the things about me is that i like to understand things from ground up and that motivated me to devote an entire part of this book to this which i call *The value proposition of data*. We'll examine this through the lens of decision theory, systems thinking, and the technological forces that have widened the gap between data-mature and data-immature organizations. This section takes a deeper look at mechanisms with which data, becomes information becomes valuable. It's my perspective that this is a great foundation to have for any data professional. The second part asks the question that most managers and self proclaimed *data-driven* organization ask themselves everyday. Because how do we integrate data into the business and redeem the latent value lying in the digital objects that flow around in the wires all around us. The third part of the book takes a look into the machinery of producing those knowledge and data-centric products as i like to call them. We go deeper into *the journey to wisdom* as some might call it, a process that have several analogies that i like to use to explain the concepts. Moving raw materials through a factory or move water through a water system can help paint the picture of what happens as the invisible electrical signals travel at the speed of light within and between hardware components. Finally the last part leaves with the message of getting practical experience and uses some success stories as inspiration to fuel this learning and work that is ahead of the student.
